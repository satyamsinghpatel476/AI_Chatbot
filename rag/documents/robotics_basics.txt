Robotics Basics

What is a Robot?
A robot is a programmable machine capable of carrying out tasks automatically or semi-automatically. Robots can sense their environment, process information, and perform actions using actuators.

Degrees of Freedom (DOF)
Degrees of freedom refer to the number of independent movements a robot can perform. For example, a robotic arm with six joints has six degrees of freedom, allowing movement in three-dimensional space.

Actuators
Actuators are components that convert energy into motion. Common actuators include electric motors, hydraulic cylinders, and pneumatic cylinders. They enable robots to move and interact with their environment.

Manipulators
Manipulators are robotic arms designed to perform tasks such as grasping, lifting, assembling, and welding. They are widely used in industrial automation.

Mobile Robots
Mobile robots can move from one location to another. Examples include autonomous vacuum cleaners, warehouse robots, and self-driving vehicles.

Industrial Robots
Industrial robots are used in manufacturing environments for repetitive and precise tasks such as assembly, painting, packaging, and welding.

Service Robots
Service robots assist humans in non-industrial settings. Examples include healthcare robots, delivery robots, educational robots, and domestic cleaning robots.

Autonomous Robots
Autonomous robots can operate without continuous human intervention by making decisions based on sensor data and programmed objectives.

Teleoperated Robots
Teleoperated robots are controlled remotely by human operators. They are commonly used in hazardous environments such as bomb disposal and space exploration.

Humanoid Robots
Humanoid robots are designed to resemble the human body structure. They are often used in research, education, and human-robot interaction studies.
